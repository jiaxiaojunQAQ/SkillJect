The Quiet Revolution in Battery Technology

For years, experts have been saying that electric vehicles were almost ready for mainstream. But many consumer's experiences told a different story. Range anxiety, long charging times and high prices kept many potential buyer's from making the switch.

Now however, things is beginning to change in a dramatic way. New solid-state batteries, which replaces the liquid electrolyte in traditional lithium-ion cells with a solid material, promises to overcome these barriers. The technology potentially offers twice the energy density, faster charge times and improved safety because the solid electrolyte doesn't catch fire like liquid ones can.

Several automakers have announced plans to introduce vehicles with solid-state batteries within the next three years. Weather these timelines will holds remains uncertain but the momentum in the industry is clearly accelerating. For consumers who have been waiting on the sidelines, the moment of decision may be arriving sooner than anyone previously anticipated.
